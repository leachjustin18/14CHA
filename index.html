In adult/neonatal circulation, poorly oxygenated blood travels from the IVC and SVC to the right atrium.
The blood then enters the right ventricle and passes through to the pulmonary arteries and into the lungs.
In the lungs, the blood is oxygenated and then enters the left atrium through the pulmonary vein.
From the left atrium, the blood passes to the left ventricle where it is pumped to the systemic circulation via the aorta to the rest of the body.
By the end of the first month after birth, the left ventricle wall is thicker than the right as it is now the main heart pump
